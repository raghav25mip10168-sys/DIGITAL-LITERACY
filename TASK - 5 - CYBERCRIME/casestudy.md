Case Study: Cyberbullying Among College Students

Cyberbullying is a form of online harassment where a person is repeatedly targeted through social media, messaging apps, or online platforms with harmful or humiliating content.
Unlike traditional bullying, it can happen anytime and reach a large audience within minutes.
Consider a realistic college scenario.
A first-year student posts a presentation video online.
A few classmates start making sarcastic comments about their appearance and speaking style.
Step-by-step, the situation escalates:
A mean comment is posted publicly.
Others join in, adding jokes, memes, or edited images.
Fake accounts are created to send direct messages with insults.
Screenshots are shared in group chats, spreading the humiliation further.
The victim may initially ignore the comments, but as the posts gain attention, the emotional impact increases.
Cyberbullying often targets students who are new, shy, perceived as “different,” or simply vulnerable in some way.
The consequences can be serious.
Victims may experience anxiety, loss of confidence, poor academic performance, and social withdrawal.
In extreme cases, mental health can be deeply affected.
For the offenders, consequences may include disciplinary action from the college, suspension, or even legal consequences under cybercrime laws.
This case highlights that responsible social media use is not just about protecting ourselves, but also about respecting others.
Online actions leave digital footprints, and a moment of “fun” can cause long-term harm to someone’s life.
